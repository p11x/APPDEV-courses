Card Shadows

Shadows create depth and hierarchy in card designs. Tailwind provides shadow utilities from shadow-sm (subtle) to shadow-2xl (prominent). Understanding when to use each level helps establish visual hierarchy.

Standard Example: All Shadow Levels

Demonstrating different shadow intensities.

<!-- No Shadow -->
<div class="bg-white rounded-lg p-6">None</div>

<!-- Small Shadow -->
<div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-sm p-6">shadow-sm</div>

<!-- Default Shadow -->
<div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow p-6">shadow</div>

<!-- Medium Shadow -->
<div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-md p-6">shadow-md</div>

<!-- Large Shadow -->
<div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-lg p-6">shadow-lg</div>

<!-- Extra Large -->
<div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-xl p-6">shadow-xl</div>

<!-- Double Extra Large -->
<div class="bg-white rounded-lg shadow-2xl p-6">shadow-2xl</div>

Real-World Example 1: Elevating Cards on Hover

Interactive cards that lift on hover for better feedback.

<div class="
    bg-white rounded-xl p-6
    shadow-md hover:shadow-xl
    transition-all duration-300
    hover:-translate-y-1
">
    <h3 class="text-lg font-bold">Hover Card</h3>
    <p class="text-gray-600 mt-2">
        Hover over this card to see it elevate.
    </p>
</div>

Edge Cases & Considerations

  • Use shadow-sm for large numbers of cards to reduce visual noise
  • Use shadow-lg/2xl sparingly for emphasis only
  • Consider dark mode when shadows may not be visible
  • Performance: shadows are expensive to render on mobile

Output Statement

After implementing card shadows, you will have:

  • All shadow levels from subtle to prominent
  • Interactive hover elevation effects
  • Featured/highlighted card styles
  • Proper visual hierarchy with shadows
