Sticky Positioning Modern

Sticky positioning is a hybrid of relative and fixed. Elements scroll normally until they hit a threshold, then "stick" in place.

Bootstrap Class: .sticky-top, .sticky-bottom, .sticky-sm-top (responsive).

1. How Sticky Positioning Works

position: sticky toggles between relative and fixed based on scroll position. The element sticks when it reaches the specified offset (e.g., top: 0).

2. Page-Level Sticky Navigation

The most common use: a navbar that sticks to the top on scroll.

<!-- Bootstrap navbar with sticky-top class -->
<nav class="navbar navbar-expand-lg navbar-dark bg-dark sticky-top">
  <div class="container">
    <a class="navbar-brand" href="#">Brand</a>
    <ul class="navbar-nav ms-auto">
      <li class="nav-item"><a class="nav-link" href="#">Link</a></li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</nav>

<!-- Content below the sticky nav -->
<div class="container my-5">
  <h1>Page Content</h1>
  <p>The navbar sticks to the top as you scroll.</p>
</div>

5. Responsive Sticky Classes

Sticky behavior can be limited to specific breakpoints.

ClassBehavior
.sticky-topSticky at all screen sizes
.sticky-sm-topSticky at sm (576px) and up
.sticky-md-topSticky at md (768px) and up
.sticky-lg-topSticky at lg (992px) and up
.sticky-xl-topSticky at xl (1200px) and up
.sticky-xxl-topSticky at xxl (1400px) and up
.sticky-bottomSticks to bottom of scroll container
 
<!-- Only sticky on medium screens and above -->
<div class="sticky-md-top">
  Sticks at md breakpoint and above
</div>

<!-- Sticky at bottom -->
<div class="sticky-bottom">
  Sticks to bottom of scroll container
</div>

6. Requirements & Gotchas

Requirements
  • Parent must have a scrollable overflow (or the page itself)
  • Parent must have a defined height (not auto/infinite)
  • Offset property required (top: 0, bottom: 0, etc.)
  • The sticky element must fit within its parent
Common Gotchas
  • overflow: hidden on parent breaks sticky
  • overflow: auto on parent breaks sticky
  • Sticky element taller than parent won't stick properly
  • No z-index = content may scroll over sticky element
